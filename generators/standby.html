Why Ann Arbor Homes Need Standby Generators

Michigan weather causes frequent power outages. Ice storms in winter, severe thunderstorms in summer, and aging infrastructure mean Washtenaw County residents experience an average of 3-5 outages per year. For homes with sump pumps, medical equipment, home offices, or well water, even a short outage can be catastrophic.

A standby generator eliminates these concerns. The system monitors your power 24/7 and starts automatically within 10 seconds of an outage. No manual intervention required - it works whether you're home, at work, or on vacation.

Natural Gas vs Propane Operation

Natural Gas (Recommended When Available)

If you have natural gas service, this is the ideal fuel source:

  • Unlimited runtime during extended outages
  • No refueling needed
  • No tanks to rent or maintain
  • Lower operating cost per hour
  • DTE coordinates gas meter upgrade if needed

Propane (Rural Properties)

For homes without natural gas:

  • Requires 500-1000 gallon tank
  • 48-72 hours runtime on full tank
  • Tank can be buried or above ground
  • We coordinate with local propane suppliers
  • Slightly higher power output than natural gas

Professional Installation Process

Day 1: Site Preparation

  • Pour concrete pad (or install GenPad if appropriate)
  • Set generator on pad with proper clearances
  • Install automatic transfer switch at main panel
  • Run control and power wiring in conduit

Day 2: Connections and Testing

  • Complete electrical connections
  • Gas piping connection (licensed plumber)
  • Program transfer switch settings
  • Full system test and load bank testing
  • Customer training on operation
  • Register warranty and set up monitoring

Code Requirements and Permits

All installations meet Michigan Electrical Code and local requirements:

  • Electrical permit from local municipality
  • Mechanical permit for gas connection
  • Minimum 5 feet from windows and doors
  • 18 inches from house (36 inches preferred)
  • Proper disconnect switch installation
  • Load calculation documentation

Automatic Transfer Switch Technology

The transfer switch is the brain of your backup power system:

  • Monitors utility voltage on all legs
  • Initiates generator start on power loss
  • Transfers load after generator stabilizes
  • Returns to utility when power restored
  • 2-line LCD display shows system status
  • Built-in surge protection
