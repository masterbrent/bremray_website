Professional Interlock Installation - The Smart Backup Power Solution

An interlock kit transforms your portable generator into a safe, code-compliant whole-house backup power system. For a fraction of the cost of automatic standby generators, you get reliable backup power that can run all the lights and outlets in your home.

This isn't a DIY extension cord solution. It's a professional, permanent installation that safely connects your generator to your electrical panel, allowing you to power any circuit in your home during an outage.

How Generator Interlock Kits Work

An interlock kit is a mechanical device that ensures your main breaker and generator breaker cannot be on simultaneously. This prevents dangerous backfeeding that could injure utility workers or damage equipment.

The System Components

  • Interlock plate: Mechanical sliding plate installed on your panel cover
  • Generator inlet box: Weatherproof 30 or 50-amp connection mounted outside
  • Generator breaker: New breaker in your panel for generator input
  • Heavy wiring: 6 or 8 AWG copper from inlet to panel
  • Connection cable: 50-amp cord from generator to inlet (sold separately)

Step-by-Step Operation During Power Outage

  1. Verify outage: Check neighbors to confirm utility outage
  2. Position generator: Roll out to safe location (20+ feet from house)
  3. Start generator: Let warm up for 2-3 minutes
  4. Connect cable: Plug into generator and inlet box
  5. Turn off main breaker: Disconnects from utility
  6. Slide interlock plate: Allows generator breaker to turn on
  7. Turn on generator breaker: Powers your panel
  8. Manage circuits: Turn on breakers for needed circuits

DIY generator connections are illegal and dangerous. Our professional installation ensures:

Electrical Safety

Code Compliance

Maintenance and Operation Tips

Generator Maintenance (DIY)

  • Monthly: Start and run for 15 minutes under load
  • Before each use: Check oil level
  • Seasonal: Change oil (every 50 hours)
  • Annual: Replace spark plug and air filter
  • Fuel: Use stabilizer if storing over 30 days

Operating Best Practices

  • Keep written procedure posted near panel
  • Practice switchover quarterly
  • Monitor total load - don't exceed generator capacity
  • Start large loads one at a time
  • Keep spare oil and filters on hand
  • Store extra fuel safely (follow local codes)
