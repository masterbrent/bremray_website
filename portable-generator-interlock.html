Portable Generator with Interlock - Complete Details

A professionally installed portable generator with an interlock kit provides reliable backup power at a fraction of the cost of standby systems. This setup allows you to safely power your electrical panel during an outage - you choose which circuits to run based on your generator's capacity.

Flexible options: Professional interlock installation ($1,200-$1,500) works with your existing portable generator OR we can supply a new tri-fuel generator ($900-$1,500). The 50-amp models we recommend can power all lights and outlets in your home.

How the Interlock System Works

An interlock kit creates a safe, code-compliant connection between your portable generator and home electrical panel. During an outage:

  1. Position generator: Place at least 20 feet from house (carbon monoxide safety)
  2. Connect inlet box: Plug generator into outdoor inlet using included cable
  3. Start generator: Let it warm up and stabilize
  4. Switch interlock: Move interlock plate to generator position (prevents backfeeding)
  5. Manage loads: Turn on breakers for circuits you want to power

What We Install

Professional Interlock Kit System

  • Interlock device: Mechanical plate that prevents main and generator breakers from being on simultaneously
  • Generator inlet box: Weatherproof outdoor connection point (30 or 50 amp)
  • Generator breaker: Dedicated breaker in your panel for generator input
  • Heavy-duty wiring: From inlet box to panel (typically 30-50 amp circuit)
  • Proper grounding: Ensures safe operation

Installation Process

Quick and straightforward - typically completed in 3-4 hours:

  1. Assessment: Evaluate your panel and determine best inlet location
  2. Install inlet box: Mount weatherproof connection point outside
  3. Run wiring: Connect inlet to panel (through wall/basement)
  4. Install interlock: Mount mechanical interlock on panel
  5. Add breaker: Install dedicated generator breaker
  6. Test system: Verify proper operation and safety
  7. Training: Show you how to safely operate during outage

Safety and Code Compliance

DIY generator connections are dangerous and illegal. Our professional installation ensures:

  • No backfeeding: Interlock prevents sending power to utility lines
  • Proper grounding: Protects against electrical faults
  • Permit and inspection: Meets all local codes
  • Insurance compliance: Documented professional installation
  • Carbon monoxide safety: Proper placement guidance

Maintenance and Operation

Regular Maintenance (DIY)

  • Monthly test run for 15 minutes
  • Check oil before each use
  • Stabilize fuel if storing over 30 days
  • Annual oil change and spark plug
  • Keep clean and covered when not in use

During an Outage

  1. Wait for power to fully fail (avoid brownout damage)
  2. Turn off main breaker
  3. Roll generator outside (minimum 20 feet from house)
  4. Start generator and let stabilize
  5. Connect to inlet box
  6. Switch interlock to generator position
  7. Turn on selected circuit breakers
  8. Monitor loads - adjust as needed

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this really safe?
Yes, when professionally installed. The interlock kit physically prevents backfeeding, making it impossible to energize utility lines. This protects utility workers and meets all electrical codes.

Can I install this myself?
No. Michigan requires a licensed electrician for panel work. DIY installation is illegal, dangerous, and voids insurance coverage. The permit and inspection process requires professional installation.

How long does the generator last?
With proper maintenance, a quality portable generator lasts 10-15 years. The interlock kit is mechanical and essentially permanent - no electronics to fail.

What about noise?
Portable generators are loud (65-75 dB). Similar to a lawn mower. Consider neighbor proximity when choosing placement. Some models have "eco" modes that reduce noise during light loads.

Can I run my central AC?
Not typically. Central AC requires 5,000-7,000 watts alone. However, window units and mini-splits work fine. The 50-amp generators can handle one small central AC (2-3 ton) if you manage other loads carefully.

What if I have a 100-amp panel?
No problem. The interlock works with any panel that has available breaker spaces. We install on 100-amp and 200-amp panels regularly.

Do you service generators?
We focus on the electrical installation. For generator maintenance, we can recommend trusted local small engine shops.
