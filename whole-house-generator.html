Automatic Standby Generators

Power outages disrupt everything - from work-from-home setups to medical equipment, refrigerated food to heating and cooling. A whole house generator provides seamless backup power, starting automatically when the grid fails. Here's how these systems work and what's involved in installation.

How Automatic Generators Work

The system continuously monitors your utility power. When an outage is detected, the automatic transfer switch disconnects from the grid and starts the generator. Within 10-20 seconds, your home is running on generator power. When utility power returns, the system automatically switches back and shuts down the generator.

Generator Sizing Guidelines

Proper sizing ensures your generator can handle your home's electrical load:

  • Homes under 2,000 sq ft: 16-20kW typically sufficient
  • 2,000-3,000 sq ft homes: 22kW recommended
  • Homes over 3,000 sq ft: 24-26kW or larger
  • Additional considerations: Pools, hot tubs, and multiple HVAC systems require upsizing

Fuel Options - Natural Gas vs Propane

Natural Gas (If You Have It)

  • Unlimited runtime - no refueling
  • Slightly less power output (10% reduction)
  • No tanks to rent or own

Propane (Rural Properties)

  • Need 500+ gallon tank minimum
  • Must monitor fuel levels during outages
  • Full power output

Transfer Switches - The Brain of the System

Automatic Transfer Switch (Standard)

  • Monitors utility power 24/7
  • Starts generator within 10 seconds of outage
  • Switches back when power returns
  • No action required from you
  • Included with whole-house generators

Manual Transfer Switch (Portable Generators)

  • You flip the switch during outages
  • Prevents dangerous backfeeding
  • Required by code for portable connections

Load Management Modules

Smart option for smaller generators:

  • Automatically sheds non-essential loads
  • Lets 22kW generator act like 26kW
  • Manages AC units, water heaters, dryers

What About Solar + Battery?

Great question. Solar + battery backup costs 3-4x more than a generator and won't run your AC or heat for days. Generators are still the best solution for extended outages. You can always add solar later.

What to Plan For

  • Annual maintenance: Required for warranty and reliability
  • Exercise cycles: Weekly self-test keeps the system ready
  • Battery replacement: Every few years
  • Clearances: Tree trimming and placement considerations
  • Sound: Similar to a central AC unit; placement matters

Maintenance Reality

Generators need regular service to stay reliable:

  • Weekly self-test: Runs automatically for 12 minutes
  • Annual service: Oil change, filter replacement, battery check
  • After outage service: Needed after 24+ hours runtime
  • 5-year major service: Valve adjustment, deeper inspection
